About Us

We started of with a simple question, Which animal’s design can be replicated in order to create the most energy-efficient underwater robot?It was observed that many aquatic animals possessed traits and movements that allowed them to travel effectively in water. However, it was unknown which animal’s design could be replicated in order to create the most effective underwater robot. Creating an energy-efficient underwater robot is important because the technology can be used for a variety of purposes, including researching marine wildlife, harnessing ocean currents for energy, and exploring underwater areas. Many research companies have created a variety of different animal-based robots to fulfill a variety of different underwater research tasks. For example, the U-CAT underwater robotic turtle was designed for the purpose of exploring underwater shipwrecks and was given four highly-maneuverable flippers to do so. Alternatively, robots like the Crabster CR200, which were designed to navigate the ocean floor, take inspiration from different animals like crabs and lobsters, both of which are skilled at navigating the terrain. However, many of these robots were not designed with speed or energy consumption in mind. In order to create the most energy-efficient robot, we must first determine which animals are the most energy-efficient swimmers. After discovering this animal, we will identify which features allow it to achieve its efficiency. From that point, we will start to design a bot in “Auto Desk” or any other cadding app. This model will be shown to the judges. We will also build a prototype of the best robot. It is hypothesized that a jellyfish-themed robot would be the most efficient swimmer because jellyfish expend a low amount of energy compared to many other aquatic animals. The data from our experiment will be useful because being able to create an energy-efficient robot will allow for underwater research to occur in a fast and resource-friendly manner.
